AI Inference Inflection Research

The core thesis is that AI inference is moving from a sidecar workload to the dominant compute and capex driver. The best risk-adjusted expressions are the infrastructure layers that make inference possible at scale: power, cooling, networking, memory, advanced packaging, and the platforms that monetize inference demand directly.

Last updated: 2026-03-29

Current stance: Bullish on infrastructure and systems; selective on direct app-layer beneficiaries.

Predictions To Track

OPEN | Target 2026-12-31

Inference capex stays elevated through year-end 2026

Hyperscalers will keep AI capex elevated and continue framing demand around inference, reasoning and agentic workloads rather than just training.

Evidence to watch: Quarterly capex guidance, token-growth commentary, inference product revenue and utilization remarks.

How it could fail: Capex is cut materially or managements shift from shortage language to oversupply language.

OPEN | Target 2026-12-31

Power and cooling outperform as the cleanest second-order beneficiaries

Power, cooling and electrical vendors will prove to be cleaner beneficiaries of inference growth than many direct app-layer AI names.

Evidence to watch: Backlog growth, margin expansion and AI-specific order commentary from infrastructure vendors.

How it could fail: Order growth decelerates sharply while app-layer monetizers capture most of the economics.

OPEN | Target 2026-12-31

Networking and memory remain bottlenecks, not commoditized sidecars

Inference growth will keep high-performance networking and memory strategic rather than reducing them to commodity line items.

Evidence to watch: AI networking revenue disclosures, HBM supply commentary, optical adoption and cluster-scale design announcements.

How it could fail: Inference architectures localize enough that bandwidth and memory bottlenecks ease materially.

OPEN | Target 2026-12-31

Utility and transmission event signals remain tradable

AI-related utility backlog, load-contract and transmission-approval events will continue to create medium-horizon trading setups.

Evidence to watch: New data-center tariffs, contracted-load updates, PJM awards and utility IR disclosures.

How it could fail: New events stop showing positive post-disclosure abnormal returns versus XLU.
